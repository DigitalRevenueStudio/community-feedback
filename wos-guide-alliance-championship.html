Overview

What Is Alliance Championship?

Alliance Championship is a seasonal event running across 8 weeks with weekly battles on Fridays. Your alliance competes against rival alliances for control of 16 structures on the world map — 12 Fortresses and 4 Strongholds.

Battles last 2 hours. To capture a structure, your alliance must hold it for 30 continuous minutes, or control it when the battle timer ends. Held structures provide ongoing buffs to all alliance members while you control them.

Registration: R4+ leadership must register your alliance for specific target structures in advance of each weekly battle. Members cannot choose targets individually — coordinate with your alliance leadership.

Structure Types

Fortresses and Strongholds

Fortresses (12) — The primary contested structures. Each provides defensive and offensive buffs to alliance members. Spread across the state map at varying strategic positions.

Strongholds (4) — Higher-value structures providing stronger buffs. More heavily contested and typically targeted by the top alliances on the server.

The buffs from held structures stack — the more structures your alliance controls, the stronger every member becomes. Alliances that dominate Alliance Championship become significantly harder to fight against in other events like SvS.

Note: Do not confuse Alliance Championship (Fortress Battle) with Foundry Battle. They are completely separate events. Foundry Battle is bi-weekly on Sundays, 30v30, facility capture for Arsenal Points. Fortress Battle is weekly on Fridays, seasonal, structure control for ongoing buffs.

F2P Strategy

Contributing as F2P Player

As a lower-power player, your role in Fortress Battle is support rather than leading attacks. Three ways to contribute effectively:

Reinforcing held structures — when your alliance successfully captures a Fortress, reinforce it immediately. Your troops contribute to the defence without needing to win fights solo.

Disrupting enemy reinforcements — attack enemy troops moving between structures on the map. You do not need to win — slowing reinforcements gives your alliance more time to capture.

Consistent participation — showing up every week matters more than winning individual fights. Alliance Championship is a season — sustained presence builds rankings over time.

Shield management: If you are significantly weaker than the competition on your server, activate a Shield before the battle phase begins to protect your troops. Losing troops permanently to a one-sided fight is not a worthwhile contribution.
