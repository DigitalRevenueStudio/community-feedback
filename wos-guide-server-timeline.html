Server Timeline —
What Unlocks and When

Whiteout Survival operates on a server timeline that progresses through generations and unlocks new content at specific intervals. Understanding where your server is in its timeline lets you plan your hero investments, anticipate upcoming events, and avoid wasting resources on content that is not yet available to you.

Generations

Hero Generation Schedule

New hero generations unlock on a rolling schedule, typically every 40–80 days in the early game, though timing varies significantly across servers and can be adjusted by game updates. Each generation introduces stronger heroes and new events. Your SvS matchmaking is also influenced by your server's generation — you are generally matched with servers of similar age.

G1
Generation 1 — Day 0
Molly, Sergey, Bahiti available. Jeronimo & Natalia P2W only. Hall of Chiefs runs 3× featuring Jeronimo. A common F2P approach: save Gold Shards for Gen 2 Alonso.
G2
Generation 2 — typically Day 40–80
Flint (wheel), Philly (HoC), Alonso. Gold Shards → Alonso 4 stars. Flint → Lucky Wheel only. HoC now features Philly.
G3
Generation 3 — typically Day 120–160
Mia and Logan arrive. Mia stays relevant to Gen 6+. Logan excels in defensive roles. Safe Exclusive Gear targets.
G4+
Generation 4–6 — timing varies significantly
Reina, Lynn, Gwen, Wayne, Hector. Continue Lucky Wheel strategy. Alonso and Mia investments remain competitive throughout.

A common F2P strategy: Aim to acquire two heroes per generation through Lucky Wheel spins and saved Legendary Shards. This pace generally keeps your roster competitive without requiring spending, though priorities vary by server and alliance.

Furnace Milestones

Key Furnace Levels and What They Unlock

F7
Alliance tab unlocks. Join an alliance immediately. The benefits start the moment you join.
F9
Required for many gift codes. Resource events become more accessible.
F16
Foundry Battle unlocks — one of the highest-value alliance events. See the full Foundry Battle guide →
F19
The Labyrinth unlocks. Weekly reset event with excellent token rewards including Mithril and Fire Crystal Shards.
F26
Advanced combat content. Competition increases as more players reach late-game troop tiers and Fire Crystal upgrades begin to create meaningful power gaps.
F30
Fire Crystal content — major late-game progression system unlocks. Target milestone for serious F2P players.

Furnace first, always. Every other building and system in the game is gated behind your Furnace level. Never let anything else take priority over your next Furnace upgrade.

Planning Ahead

How to Use the Timeline as F2P

The most common planning mistake is over-investing in a current generation hero when the next generation is close. If Gen 2 is 2–3 weeks away, save your Gold Shards rather than spending them on Gen 1 heroes that will soon be superseded.

The same logic applies to Exclusive Gear — only invest in heroes you are confident will remain relevant for at least 2 to 3 generations. Alonso, Mia, and Logan are the safest long-term investments in the early-to-mid game.

Watch your alliance's server announcements and the in-game news feed for generation unlock notices. Planning your Hall of Chiefs gem saving around upcoming hero generations is one of the highest-leverage F2P decisions you can make.

⚠ F2P Priority Rule: If the next generation is within 2–3 weeks — STOP spending and save everything. Gold Shards, Gems, Skill Manuals. Every resource you hold now is worth more when spent on the next generation's stronger heroes.

Common Mistakes

Biggest Resource Mistakes

Spending shards before the next generation — Gold Shards spent on a Gen 1 hero the week before Gen 2 unlocks is one of the most painful F2P mistakes. Check your server timeline before every major resource spend.

Investing Exclusive Gear in short-term heroes — Exclusive Gear is expensive and takes weeks to accumulate. Only invest in heroes you are confident will remain in your lineup for 2–3+ generations.

Deprioritising Furnace upgrades — most events, unlocks, and progression systems are gated behind Furnace level. Falling behind on Furnace upgrades is one of the most common causes of progression bottlenecks in the mid-game.

Community note: Generation unlock timings vary between servers and change with game updates. The timings in this guide are approximate. Ask senior players in your alliance for your specific server's current generation pace.

Action Plan

What Should I Do Right Now?

Where you are on the server timeline changes what you should focus on. Find your stage below:

🔴 Early Server — Gen 1–2
→ Furnace first — push toward F16 for Foundry Battle unlock
→ Build Molly and Sergey — solid F2P foundation that carries through Gen 2
→ Save Gold Shards — do not spend on Gen 1 heroes if Gen 2 is within a few weeks
→ Join an active alliance — alliance events are your primary resource source at this stage
🟠 Mid Server — Gen 3–4
→ Deepen Alonso investment — aim for 4 stars if not already there
→ Start building Mia — long-term Lancer investment, relevant for multiple generations
→ Participate in Foundry Battle — if F16+ reached, this is your best weekly points event
→ Check next generation timing — if within 2–3 weeks, shift to saving mode
🟢 Late Server — Gen 5–6+
→ Focus on Fire Crystal progression — this is the primary late-game power lever
→ Push toward F30 — unlocks the highest-tier content and competitive events
→ Consolidate hero investments — depth over breadth, max your core lineup before expanding
→ Alliance Championship becomes critical — structure buffs create compounding advantages at this stage
